# 📄 Case Study: Identity Theft

Identity theft is a type of cybercrime in which a person’s personal information is stolen and used without their consent, usually for financial gain or fraudulent activities. This information may include bank details, Aadhaar number, passwords, or social media credentials.

A typical identity theft scenario begins when a victim unknowingly shares sensitive information. For example, a college student receives a fake email claiming to be from a bank, asking them to update their account details. The email contains a link to a fraudulent website that looks identical to the official one. When the student enters their login credentials, the attacker gains access to their account. The attacker may then transfer money, apply for loans, or misuse the identity for illegal activities.

Identity theft commonly targets students, elderly individuals, and people who are less aware of online security practices. Students are especially vulnerable due to frequent use of digital platforms and limited awareness about phishing scams.

The consequences of identity theft can be severe. Victims may suffer financial losses, damage to their credit score, and emotional stress. In some cases, their identity may be used for criminal activities, leading to legal complications.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while sharing personal information online and verifying the authenticity of websites and emails before taking any action.
